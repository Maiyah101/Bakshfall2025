Understanding Mushrooms

The Hidden Kingdom

Mushrooms are the fruiting bodies of fungi — an entirely separate kingdom from plants and animals. Some fungi are edible, some medicinal, and others mysterious or dangerous. Their variety is astonishing, from the everyday button mushroom to the rare and stunning blue Mycena.

"Fungi are the grand recyclers of the planet and the vanguard species in habitat restoration."
— Paul Stamets

Why Study Mushrooms?

They play a crucial ecological role by decomposing organic matter and forming symbiotic relationships with plants. Studying fungi helps us better understand ecosystem health and discover new medicines.

Beyond the Forest Floor

Mushrooms have found their way into food, medicine, fashion, and even architecture. Cultures around the world use mushrooms in art, dyeing, and rituals. Some believe fungi can help us build a more sustainable future through biodegradable materials and mycelium-based construction.
