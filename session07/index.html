About the Devil’s Fingers Fungus

Introduction

Clathrus archeri, or Devil's Fingers, is a bizarre-looking fungus originally native to Australia and New Zealand, now found in Europe and North America. It begins life as a gelatinous white "egg" before bursting open to reveal bright red tentacle-like arms.

Appearance and Smell

When mature, the fungus displays 4–7 bright red “fingers” covered in a foul-smelling spore slime. This slime smells like rotting meat, attracting flies that help spread its spores.

Habitat and Spread

Devil’s Fingers grow on decaying wood and leaf litter. Its introduction to other continents is believed to be accidental—likely through contaminated supplies during World War I.
